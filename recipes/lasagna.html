Lasagna Della Mamma

Description

A heartwarming classic straight from the family kitchen! This recipe layers tender pasta sheets with rich, slow-cooked meat sauce, creamy béchamel, and a generous topping of melted cheese. Perfect for Sunday dinners or special occasions, this dish captures the comforting taste of home in every bite. Buon appetito!

Ingredients

  • For the Meat Sauce:
    • 500g (1 lb) ground beef or a mix of beef and pork
    • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
    • 2 garlic cloves, minced
    • 1 large carrot, finely grated
    • 2 celery sticks, finely diced
    • 800g (28 oz) canned crushed tomatoes
    • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
    • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
    • 1 teaspoon dried basil
    • 1 bay leaf
    • 1/2 cup red wine (optional)
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • For the Béchamel Sauce:
    • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
    • 4 tablespoons all-purpose flour
    • 3 cups whole milk
    • A pinch of ground nutmeg
    • Salt and pepper to taste
  • For the Lasagna:
    • 12–16 sheets of lasagna pasta (fresh or dried)
    • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
    • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
    • Fresh basil leaves for garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Meat Sauce:
    1. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat.
    2. Add the onion, garlic, carrot, and celery. Sauté until softened, about 5 minutes.
    3. Add the ground beef (or mix of beef and pork) and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon.
    4. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 1–2 minutes.
    5. Add the crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, bay leaf, and red wine (if using). Season with salt and pepper.
    6. Reduce heat to low and let the sauce simmer gently for 30–40 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    7. Remove the bay leaf and set the sauce aside.
  2. Prepare the Béchamel Sauce:
    1. In a medium saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat.
    2. Whisk in the flour and cook for 1–2 minutes to form a roux.
    3. Gradually add the milk while whisking constantly to prevent lumps.
    4. Cook the sauce, stirring, until it thickens (about 5–7 minutes).
    5. Season with a pinch of nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Remove from heat.
  3. Assemble the Lasagna:
    1. Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F).
    2. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish.
    3. Layer lasagna pasta sheets over the sauce.
    4. Spread a layer of meat sauce, followed by a layer of béchamel sauce.
    5. Sprinkle mozzarella and Parmesan cheese over the béchamel layer.
    6. Repeat the layering process (pasta, meat sauce, béchamel, cheese) until all ingredients are used, finishing with a cheese layer on top.
  4. Bake the Lasagna:
    1. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 25 minutes.
    2. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15–20 minutes, until the cheese is golden and bubbly.
    3. Let the lasagna cool for 10 minutes before serving.
    4. Garnish with fresh basil leaves if desired and serve warm.
